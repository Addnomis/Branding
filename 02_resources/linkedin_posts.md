## Post 13
**Date:** 2 years ago
**Content:** Recent exploration into Hyper-resolutions with images above 3 billion pixels with #stablediffusion, bypassing the capabilities of programs like Photoshop where procedural computation is the only viable tool.

The basis of AI image generators is 'creating something, from nothing'. These images are snapshots of a larger hyper-tile, generated and controlle by gestures from #procreate digital painting. Coined, 'hyper-resolution' is a recursive image state where a 'hyper-tile' represents an original pixel where new information is guided from, not generated from, original information.

## Post 14
**Date:** 2 years ago
**Content:** Some process work leveraging custom #python and #stablediffusion scrips to create massive artwork for a translucent custom 75'x12' canopy. Final image will be 349,000px by 44,100px at 300DPI, an impossible task without the computational design approach.

The process starts with a synthographic using #midjourney #procreate and #ControlNet. This image is then outpainted and then procedurally cropped/trimmed and re scaled and subdivided into 512x512 tiles to upscale.

After the initial upscale, they are divided into strips for subdivision and upscale and stitched together for fabrication onto the 12'x2' canopy panels. Each image requires specific 6" overlap with 4" crop and .5" bleeds and post-processing cropping to sure image continuity across the panels after printing and decal application. Once the images are complete the digital twin Revit model is procedurally textured and updated for documentation.
