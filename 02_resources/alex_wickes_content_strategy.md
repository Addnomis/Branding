## 📝 Writing Tips for LinkedIn

### **Hook Patterns That Work**
- **Unpopular opinion:** [Contrarian take]
- **Real talk:** [Honest observation]
- **Most architects [X]. I [Y].**
- **The biggest challenge isn't [expected]. It's [unexpected].**
- **After [time period] doing [work], here's what I learned:**

### **Body Structure**
1. **Hook** (1-2 sentences) - Stop the scroll
2. **Context** (2-3 sentences) - Set up the insight
3. **Value** (3-5 bullets or sentences) - Deliver the goods
4. **CTA** (1 question) - Invite engagement

### **Formatting Best Practices**
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Use line breaks liberally
- Bullets (→) for scanability
- Bold sparingly for emphasis
- One emoji max per post (if any)
- Always end with a question

### **Tone Guidelines**
- **Confident, not arrogant** - "Here's what worked" not "I'm the best"
- **Technical, not jargon-heavy** - Explain concepts clearly
- **Direct, not defensive** - State positions clearly
- **Professional, not stuffy** - Conversational but polished
- **Helpful, not promotional** - Share value, not ads

## 🚫 What to Avoid

### **Content to Never Post**
- Personal vulnerability/emotional posts
- Complaints about work/industry
- Political opinions
- Religious content (except professionally relevant)
- Memes/jokes (unless highly relevant)
- "Hustle culture" content
- Generic motivational quotes
- Overly sales-y promotion

### **Engagement to Avoid**
- Arguments in comments
- Negative comments on others' posts
- Calling out individuals/firms
- Responding to trolls
- Over-commenting (looks desperate)
- Connecting without personalization
